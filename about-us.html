Our editorial method aligns with close reading practices: claim, evidence, counter-reading, and acknowledgment of uncertainty. Sources and verification

Our sourcing follows established tiers: primary text as foundation, reputable reference works for context, library catalogs for research guidance, and peer-reviewed scholarship when available. We prioritize sources that provide verifiable information and acknowledge their own limitations.

Reference sources include Wikipedia's McCarthy overview for basic biographical and bibliographical information, Britannica's biography for established critical perspectives, and the Library of Congress for research methodology and primary source guidance.

We distinguish between different types of sources and their appropriate uses. Reference works provide starting points and basic context, while scholarly sources offer detailed analysis and interpretive frameworks. We encourage readers to move from general reference materials to specialized academic resources as their interest and research needs develop.

When citing external sources, we focus on their utility for further research rather than treating them as final authorities. Our goal is to provide readers with reliable starting points for their own investigation rather than comprehensive coverage of all available scholarship.

Accessibility and constraints

This site uses static HTML and CSS without JavaScript, designed for fast international access and broad device compatibility. We prioritize semantic HTML5 landmarks, keyboard navigation support, and reduced motion preferences to ensure judge holden interpretation resources remain accessible to diverse users.

Technical choices reflect our commitment to content accessibility: focus-visible styling for keyboard users, sufficient color contrast for readability, and responsive design that works across screen sizes and connection speeds. The site loads quickly even on slower international connections, supporting our goal of global accessibility.

We avoid images, external dependencies, and complex interactive elements that might create barriers for users with different technical setups or accessibility needs. This approach aligns with our editorial focus on textual analysis and ensures that content remains the primary emphasis.

The static design also supports long-term stability and archival access, important considerations for educational and research resources. Readers can expect consistent functionality and content availability regardless of changing web technologies or platform updates.
